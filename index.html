About Me

At present, I'm a computer scientist at the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory. Before that, I earned a Ph.D. in the Department of Computer Engineering at Rochester Institute of Technology in 2024. I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Dongfang Liu and Dr. Michael Zuzak. I also received the degree of Bachelor of Science in Liberal Arts & Sciences, majoring in Mathematics and Statistics, from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) in May 2021.

I am continually seeking long-term collaborators from U.S. academic institutions, who share the same research interests and academic perspectives, especially for DOD proposals such as DARPA, ONR, etc.

Research Interests

My research interests include machine learning, artificial intelligence, and high-dimensional statistical estimation, ranging from applications to theory.

  • Computer Vision: Image Classification, Object Detection, Image Segmentation, Single and Multiple Object Tracking
  • Remote Sensing: Far Seas Geo-localization, Ship Re-identification
  • Security: Trustworthy Machine Learning, Attack and Countermeasure for Perception System
  • Embodied AI: Visual Navigation, Multi-agent and Multi-objective Collaborations
  • AI for Science: AI for Antenna, AI for Radar, AI for Electromagnetism, AI for Maritime Application
